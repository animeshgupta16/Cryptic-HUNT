01 Overview — What This File Is

This is a public-record dossier for a False Claims Act qui tam case brought by two former employees of Gramercy Surgery Center, an outpatient facility in Houston, Texas, against Columbia/HCA. The relators — Scott Barrett and Marie Goodwin — alleged improper Medicare co-pay waivers, mis-coding of uncovered procedures, pricing and referral arrangements benefiting physician owners, and retaliation after they raised concerns internally.

Read carefully: an allegation is not a finding. A parent company's nationwide settlement is not the same thing as a verdict in this case. This file separates the two — and so should you, once you get to the Terminal.

The case never reached a fraud trial. It was narrowed twice — once on the pleadings, once on summary judgment — before it closed. The two rulings that did that narrowing are the heart of this archive, and of what it takes to unseal it. Read Rulings slowly. Every word of it is load-bearing.

02 Persons of Interest

Scott Barrett Administrator · Relator

Became Gramercy's administrator in late 1995. Filed the sealed qui tam action in March 1998. Settled his own employment claims privately in September 2004 — terms were never made public.

Marie Goodwin Business Operations Manager · Relator

Ran Gramercy's business office from 1993 to January 1997. Logged pricing concessions and raised concerns up the chain starting in 1996. Fired January 2, 1997, after a dispute over year-end reports. Her retaliation claim later failed for lack of a proven causal link.

Carol Simons Successor Administrator

Took over both the Travis and Gramercy sites in December 1996 after Barrett's firing. Fired Goodwin following an escalating argument over reporting duties on December 31, 1996 and January 2, 1997.

Greg Beasley Regional VP, Ambulatory Surgery

Visited Gramercy in May 1996 and joined a conference call where co-pay waivers, doctor demands, and pricing were discussed — later cited as the strongest evidence of protected reporting.

Kirk Tenkhoff Regional Compliance Director

Present on the May 1996 call. Later testified he told the relators that discounting co-pays and deductibles was improper, and that he planned to consult counsel and the CFOs.

Pam Ashworth Regional CFO

Goodwin raised the overpayment holding account with her in September 1996. Ashworth directed that future overpayments go back to insurers, but didn't address funds already transferred.

Exhibit A — Sealed Complaint Filed March 1998

Alleges routine co-pay waivers, mis-coded procedures, discounted or free cases for referring physicians, an equipment-lease arrangement, and retained insurance overpayments.

Exhibit B — D.C. MDL Opinion Feb. 24, 2003

251 F. Supp. 2d 28. Holds that kickback theories and implied certification can, in principle, support False Claims Act liability — but dismisses the fraud counts without prejudice for lack of Rule 9(b) particularity.
